WHAT'S COVERED?

In the initial phase of coverage, NHI Bahamas will cover “ primary care” , which is essentially care that is provided to you by a family doctor or other health professional at the front line of care. This includes:

1

Promotion: helping people to improve their overall health and wellbeing by promoting healthy behaviours and creative supportive environments for people to increase control over their health

a.

Examples

i.

Counselling for breastfeeding mothers

ii.

Monitoring and promoting nutrition, education and hygiene amongst children and
young adults

iii.

Awareness initiatives to inform Bahamians about the benefits of using health services
for appropriate reasons

iv.

Violence prevention programmes

vi.

Conducting baseline health assessment studies to monitor health outcomes long-term

2

Prevention: health care measures taken to prevent illness and disease and to best position persons for a healthy future. This also includes early screening for disease.

a.

Examples

i.

Counselling for breastfeeding mothers

ii.

Monitoring and promoting nutrition, education and hygiene amongst children and
young adults

iii.

Awareness initiatives to inform Bahamians about the benefits of using health services
for appropriate reasons

iv.

Violence prevention programmes

vi.

Conducting baseline health assessment studies to monitor health outcomes long-term

3

Care: medical services to maintain and improve health, including treatment of common health conditions.

a.

Examples

i.

Counselling for breastfeeding mothers

ii.

Monitoring and promoting nutrition, education and hygiene amongst children and
young adults

iii.

Awareness initiatives to inform Bahamians about the benefits of using health services
for appropriate reasons

iv.

Violence prevention programmes

vi.

Conducting baseline health assessment studies to monitor health outcomes long-term
